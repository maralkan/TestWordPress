I decided to switch up my reading. Instead of reading Jeremiah, I will read the gospels.\u00a0I realized late last night that I need to have more faith to be able to be strong in all situations. (Romans 10:17 NIV)<\/p>\n

\n

And a voice came from heaven: “You are my Son, whom I love; with you I am well pleased.”\u00a0\u2014 Mark 1:11 NIV<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

A Christian is a follower of Jesus. Therefore, an imitator of Jesus. God was well pleased with Jesus. Therefore, as I imitate Christ, God will be pleased with me just as Jesus pleased him in his time. What’s crazy is that God has long encouraged prophets to remain steadfast and to know his heart. But this time it was different. This time, God was pleased with Jesus before he had started his ministry! Already, this distinguishes Jesus from all of the prophets.<\/p>\n

The question here is, how do I imitate Christ? Simple, I must learn what Jesus did and taught.\u00a0How do I learn that? I can’t rely on anyone to teach me. I must do it for myself. It’s my personal relationship with God.<\/p>\n

So then, what are the teachings of Jesus? I guess I’m going to learn more and more as I continue to read the Bible.<\/p>\n

Application for Today<\/h3>\n

Today, it seems, I will have to follow the example of Christ.<\/p>\n

\n

\n

Love must be sincere.<\/strong> Hate what is evil; cling to what is good. Be devoted to one another in love. Honor one another above yourselves.<\/strong>\u00a0Never be lacking in zeal, but keep your spiritual fervor, serving the Lord. Be joyful in hope, patient in affliction, faithful in prayer. Share with the Lord\u2019s people who are in need. Practice hospitality.<\/p>\n

Bless those who persecute you; bless and do not curse. Rejoice with those who rejoice; mourn with those who mourn. Live in harmony with one another. Do not be proud, but be willing to associate with people of low position. Do not be conceited.<\/p>\n

Do not repay anyone evil for evil. Be careful to do what is right in the eyes of everyone. If it is possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone.<\/strong>\u00a0Do not take revenge, my dear friends, but leave room for God\u2019s wrath, for it is written: \u201cIt is mine to avenge; I will repay,\u201d says the Lord. On the contrary:<\/p>\n

\u201cIf your enemy is hungry, feed him;
\nif he is thirsty, give him something to drink.
\nIn doing this, you will heap burning coals on his head.\u201d
\nDo not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good.\u00a0\u2014\u00a0Romans 12:9-21 NIV<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

and<\/p>\n

\n

We love because he first loved us. Whoever claims to love God yet hates a brother or sister is a liar<\/strong>. For whoever does not love their brother and sister, whom they have seen, cannot love God<\/strong>, whom they have not seen. And he has given us this command<\/strong>: Anyone who loves God must also love their brother and sister.\u00a0\u2014\u00a01 John 4:19-21 NIV<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

Christ taught many times throughout his time on earth and through his apostles’ writings that we must have love for one another. I can imitate Christ by having love for my brothers (adelphoi<\/em> means brothers and sisters). How can I love my brothers? I can definitely start by changing my character. So many times, I thought that everyone else had to change because I was doing everything right. I love people, I give sacrificially, I do\u00a0more things (in my opinion) than others. But that doesn’t make me righteous. When someone ignores me I get butthurt and I tend to pull away my love because I think it’s not worth it to love a person who will not appreciate my love. But Christ commands us to love as he did. He loved despite the rejection of the apostles when he was turned in to the authorities. He loved always. So I must also always love even in the face of rejection. That way, I will overcome evil with good.
