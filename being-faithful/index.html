Being Faithful

by marvin

Today, I’m continuing to read Mark’s account of the ministry of Jesus. I think it’s interesting how Jesus had a heart to help everyone but wanted to remain on the down low until it was his time. Curiously, there’s the aspect of Jesus that many people can often overlook.

Prayer and Reading

Jesus had a daily relationship with God. As I learn to imitate Christ, I must have a daily relationship with God. How can I be best friends with someone if I don’t talk to them? How can I say I love God if I don’t talk to him or read his Word? Again, I can reference Romans 10:17 NIV.

Jesus had a daily relationship with God. But another thing to note, is that he always had time with God before he did anything throughout the day. We don’t have his prayer but it’s probably for strength. Why would anyone want to love people who are only interested in you because you provide some sort of advantage and no real friendship?¬†Jesus prayed and then he decided to go elsewhere so that others may hear his preaching. So far, the only thing we know is that he has preached about the coming of the kingdom of God. And since the kingdom of God has come near, people must repent and believe that it is good news!

But what does this message that he preaches have to do with Christ? In a future post, I will talk about the kingdom of God in further detail. But it leaves me with obvious impression that Jesus is not preaching about himself. He is preaching about something important that is to come and is very near.

Purpose

When Jesus told his first disciples to follow him, he talked to fishermen with words that they knew about. He used their profession to explain to them that he has a new purpose for them. Now, these are his disciples. Today, people think that a disciple is so much more than a Christian. A Christian, for some reason, means that you go to church and have some sort of intellectual belief in a supernatural being that he/she calls God. But a disciple is a follower, a student, a friend, an apprentice. How are they different? Is a disciple more faithful than a Christian? Is a Christian then, some term that you can use freely to say that you believe in a supernatural being?
